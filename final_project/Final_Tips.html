Baking Tips & Trouble shooting

Having problems and confusions while baking? Your on the right page.

Even the best bakers make mistakes — and that’s how we learn! This page is your go-to guide for solving common baking problems and picking up simple tricks to make every batch better. From cakes that sink to cookies that spread too much, Sally’s Bakery has you covered with easy fixes and pro tips to keep your confidence (and your oven) warm.

1. Why Did My Cake Sink?

A sunken cake can be disappointing, but it’s usually easy to fix next time!

  • Avoid opening the oven door too early — that rush of cool air can collapse your cake.
  • Make sure your oven is fully preheated before baking.
  • Check that your baking powder or soda isn’t expired — fresh leavening agents make all the difference!

2. How to Get Chewy Cookies

If your cookies turn out fluffy instead of chewy, try these quick adjustments:

  • Use more brown sugar than white sugar — it adds moisture and chewiness.
  • Slightly underbake your cookies; they’ll firm up as they cool.
  • Chill your dough before baking to control spreading and texture.

3.Best Substitutions If You Run Out of Eggs

No eggs? No problem! Here are a few reliable swaps that work in most recipes:

  • ¼ cup unsweetened applesauce = 1 egg (adds moisture and softness)
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water = 1 egg (great for structure)
  • ¼ cup mashed banana = 1 egg (adds flavor — perfect for muffins!)
